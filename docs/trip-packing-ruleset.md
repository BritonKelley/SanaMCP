# MEDICATION ASSESSMENT RULE SET

## CORE CATEGORY REQUIREMENTS

Every trip should include medications from these essential categories:

### 1. Analgesics (Pain/Fever)

- **Adult:** Acetaminophen (500mg tablets) + Ibuprofen (200mg tablets)
- **Pediatric:** Acetaminophen suspension (160mg/5mL) + Ibuprofen suspension (100mg/5mL)
- **Pediatric chewables:** Acetaminophen (80-160mg) + Ibuprofen (100mg)
- *Minimum quantities:* 3-10 bottles/packages per trip

### 2. Anti Infectives (Antibiotics)

- **Oral antibiotics:** Amoxicillin, Azithromycin, Ciprofloxacin, Metronidazole, Cephalexin
- **Topical antibiotics:** Bacitracin/Neomycin/Polymyxin B ointment (triple antibiotic)
- **Injectable:** Ceftriaxone (1g vials) - 8-12 vials minimum for severe infections
- *Minimum quantities:* 5-15 different antibiotic formulations

### 3. Allergy/Respiratory

- **Antihistamines:** Diphenhydramine suspension (12.5mg/5mL), Loratadine, Cetirizine
- **Inhalers:** Albuterol sulfate inhalation aerosol
- **Cough/Cold:** Dextromethorphan/Guaifenesin combinations
- **Steroids:** Prednisone/Prednisolone tablets
- *Minimum quantities:* 8-12 items, multiple formulations

### 4. Topical Medications

- **Antifungals:** Clotrimazole cream (1%) - typically 4-15 tubes
- **Anti-itch/Anti-inflammatory:** Hydrocortisone cream (1%) - typically 3-10 tubes
- **Wound care:** Triple antibiotic ointment, A&D ointment, Petroleum jelly
- **Specialized:** Permethrin cream (for scabies/lice), Diphenhydramine/Zinc cream

### 5. GI (Gastrointestinal)

- **Acid reducers:** Omeprazole, Famotidine
- **Antacids:** Calcium carbonate (Tums)
- **Anti-diarrheal:** Bismuth subsalicylate
- **Laxatives:** Bisacodyl, Docusate sodium
- *Minimum quantities:* 4-8 different GI medications

### 6. Vitamins

- **Adult multivitamins:** 15,000-25,000 tablets
- **Children's chewables with iron:** 10,000-20,000 tablets
- **Prenatal vitamins:** 2,000-4,000 tablets
- **Infant drops:** Multiple bottles (Poly-Vi-Sol or similar)
- **Vitamin A:** High-dose (25,000 IU) - 4,000-10,000 capsules for deficiency treatment

### 7. Cardiac

- **Blood pressure:** Amlodipine, Atenolol, Enalapril, Losartan, Hydrochlorothiazide, Furosemide
- **Aspirin:** 81mg tablets (for cardiac protection)
- *Minimum quantities:* 5-10 different cardiac medications

## COMMON MEDICATION VOLUME BENCHMARKS

### High-Volume Items (appear on every trip)

- Acetaminophen: 5-8 different formulations
- Ibuprofen: 4-6 different formulations
- Diphenhydramine: 6-10 different formulations
- Clotrimazole cream: 6-15 tubes
- Hydrocortisone cream: 4-10 tubes
- Triple antibiotic ointment: 5-15 tubes
- Amoxicillin: 5-15 packages (various strengths)

### Medium-Volume Items

- Cetirizine/Loratadine: 4-8 items
- Azithromycin: 2-8 packages
- Ciprofloxacin: 2-4 packages
- Metronidazole: 2-5 packages
- Omeprazole/Famotidine: 3-7 packages

## SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS

### Pediatric Coverage

- Must have liquid/suspension forms for all major categories
- Chewable tablets as alternatives
- Appropriate dosing for infants (drops) and children (suspensions)

### Injectable Medications

- **Ceftriaxone (1g vials):** 8-12 vials minimum for severe infections
- **Sodium Chloride 0.9%:** For dilution of injectable medications

### Region-Specific Medications

**Malaria-Endemic Regions** (Required for trips to Sub-Saharan Africa, parts of South/Southeast Asia, Central/South America):
- **Treatment:** Artemether/Lumefantrine (multiple packages)
- **Examples of endemic countries:** Uganda, Kenya, Tanzania, Madagascar, Cambodia, Thailand (rural areas), parts of India, Honduras, Haiti, Dominican Republic (rural areas)
- Verify current malaria risk for specific destination

**Parasitic Treatment** (Required for trips to regions with high intestinal parasite prevalence):
- **Albendazole:** For roundworm, hookworm, whipworm infections - typically 5-20 packages
- **Required for:** Most of Sub-Saharan Africa, South/Southeast Asia, rural Central/South America
- **High-risk countries:** Uganda, Kenya, Tanzania, Madagascar, India, Bangladesh, Cambodia, Vietnam, Philippines, Haiti, Honduras, Guatemala, Dominican Republic (rural areas)
- Used for mass deworming programs in areas with poor sanitation

### Expiration Date Rule

- All medications should have at least 6 months of shelf life remaining beyond the trip start date for customs clearance

---

## RED FLAGS (Indicating Incomplete Packing)

- Missing any of the 7 core categories
- No pediatric formulations
- Only one formulation of common medications (acetaminophen or ibuprofen)
- Fewer than 3 different antibiotic types
- No topical antifungals or antibiotics
- **Vitamin tablets below minimum thresholds:**
  - Adult multivitamins: <10,000 tablets
  - Children's chewable vitamins: <5,000 tablets
  - Prenatal vitamins: <1,000 tablets
- No cardiac medications
- All expiration dates within 6 months of trip start
- **Missing region-specific medications:**
  - No malaria treatment for malaria-endemic destinations
  - No albendazole for high-parasite-prevalence regions
  - No injectable antibiotics (Ceftriaxone + Sodium Chloride)
